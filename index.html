Beginner's Guide
New to K-Food?
A quick-start guide for first-timers

Korean food has gone global — here's how to explore it without being overwhelmed.

01
Start with Buldak Carbonara
The creamy carbonara version is the perfect entry point — mildly spicy, easy to make. Avoid 2× Spicy until you know what you're doing.
02
Korean "mild" ≠ American mild
The spice scale in Korea runs hotter. Original Buldak is already medium-hot by American standards. When in doubt, go one level below what you think you can handle.
03
Try kimbap — it's not sushi
If you like California rolls, kimbap is lighter, more savory, and more filling. It's one of the most approachable K-foods for newcomers.
04
No H-Mart nearby? Use Amazon
Most products on $ourceat ship nationwide via Amazon Prime. It's the easiest starting point if you're not near a Korean grocery store.
