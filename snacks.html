What Is the Korean Snack Wave?

Korean convenience store snacks (과자, gwa-ja) have been a national obsession for decades — but only recently have they reached mainstream audiences in the US and globally. The combination of TikTok unboxing videos, K-drama product placement, and wider distribution through services like Amazon and Weee! has brought Korean snacks to millions of new consumers.

What separates Korean snacks from most Western equivalents: smaller portion sizes, more varied flavor profiles (umami, sweet-savory fusion, unexpected ingredients like octopus or honey butter), and unusually high production quality for affordable price points. Korean snack brands spend significant R&D on texture — Turtle Chips' multi-layer structure is a prime example.

The brands behind these snacks — Orion, Lotte, Haitai, Nongshim — are massive conglomerates with 50-70 year histories. This isn't a trend built on novelty; it's an established snack culture that the rest of the world is just discovering.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I buy Korean snacks online in the US?

The best options are Weee! (Asian grocery delivery with widest selection), Amazon (ships to all 50 states, Prime eligible), H-Mart online (Korean specialty), and Wooltari. For popular items like Honey Butter Chips and Turtle Chips, Weee! typically has the best prices and fastest delivery in covered cities.

What are the most popular Korean snacks right now?

In 2025-2026, the Korean snacks with the most US buzz: Haitai Honey Butter Chips (the chip that caused a nationwide shortage), Orion Turtle Chips (unique multi-layer crunch), Lotte Pepero (gifted on 11/11 Pepero Day), Orion Choco Pie (a 50-year-old classic), and Nongshim Shrimp Crackers (Korea's longest-running snack brand).

What is Pepero Day?

Pepero Day is celebrated on November 11 (11/11) in Korea — the date looks like four Pepero sticks. Koreans exchange Pepero boxes as gifts, similar to Valentine's Day. It's the single biggest sales day for Lotte's Pepero brand, driving tens of millions of units sold in a single day.

Why did Honey Butter Chips cause a shortage in Korea?

When Haitai launched Honey Butter Chips in 2014, they severely underestimated demand. The sweet-salty-buttery combination was unlike anything else on the Korean snack market. Within weeks, stores sold out completely, scalpers were reselling bags for 3–5x retail price, and production couldn't keep up for months.

What do Turtle Chips taste like?

Orion Turtle Chips have a distinctive multi-layered, quadruple-stacked chip structure that creates an unusually airy and crispy texture — completely different from standard potato chips. Sweet Corn is mildly sweet and savory. Choco Churros adds chocolate and cinnamon sugar. Both flavors are highly addictive.

Are Korean snacks gluten-free or vegan?

Most traditional Korean snacks contain wheat (in the biscuit base) and are not gluten-free. Shrimp Crackers are corn-based and may be suitable for some gluten-sensitive individuals — always verify the label. Most Korean snacks also contain milk or egg, making them unsuitable for vegans. Specific vegan or gluten-free Korean snack options are limited but growing.
