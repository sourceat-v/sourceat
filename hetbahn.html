Rice, Three Times a Day

Rice is non-negotiable in Korean culture. You eat it at every meal, skip it when you're sick, and crave it when you're well. In 1996, CJ CheilJedang asked a simple question: what if you could have restaurant-quality short-grain rice in 90 seconds? The answer was Hetbahn — short-grain rice sealed under high pressure, shelf-stable for months, but indistinguishable from freshly cooked when microwaved.

Koreans resisted at first. Fresh rice is a matter of pride. But convenience always wins. By the 2010s, Hetbahn was in every Korean dorm, office, and kitchen. It didn't replace rice culture — it became part of it.

The Reaction Was Always the Same

Americans grew up with Uncle Ben's — shelf-stable, mushy, optimized for nothing except surviving in a pantry. When food creators opened a Hetbahn packet and got fluffy, properly sticky short-grain rice from a 90-second microwave, the reaction had a formula: disbelief, then a side-by-side comparison with stovetop rice, then "Why doesn't America have this?"

Each video compounded the next. By mid-2024, Hetbahn was the top-selling instant rice on Amazon — not because of a marketing campaign, but because it was genuinely better. The broader lesson: Korean convenience food isn't a compromise. It's often just better.

Start With White. Don't Stop There.

White rice is the entry point — clean, neutral, suited to whatever you're eating alongside it. Mixed Grain (잡곡) adds black rice, barley, and sorghum for a nuttier bowl with more fiber. Black Rice (흑미) runs slightly sweet and fragrant. Brown Rice (현미) is the whole-grain version that somehow stays soft.

The Cupban line is where it gets interesting. Rice with a full stew topping — kimchi jjigae, sundubu, doenjang — sealed in one cup. Two minutes in the microwave and you have a complete meal. No dishes, no prep. Then there's Sotban (솥반): a stone pot technique that produces a slightly caramelized, chewy bottom layer — nurungji (누룽지) — that Koreans are deeply nostalgic about. The instant rice that started as a convenience product became an entire cuisine.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hetbahn as good as freshly cooked rice?

Most people who try it say yes — or close enough that they can't tell the difference. CJ uses a retort cooking process under high pressure that locks in the texture of freshly cooked short-grain rice. Eat it immediately after microwaving. It won't beat a freshly cooked pot of premium Japanese rice, but it beats most home-cooked rice in terms of consistency.

How do you eat Hetbahn?

Peel back the film slightly, microwave for 90 seconds, and eat directly from the container or transfer to a bowl. Koreans eat it with side dishes (banchan), soup, or kimchi. For a first try, just eating it plain with a small pat of butter or a drizzle of sesame oil is a good starting point.

What's the difference between Hetbahn and Ottogi instant rice?

Both are high-quality instant rice. Hetbahn (CJ) tends to be slightly stickier and moister — closer to the texture of freshly cooked Korean rice. Ottogi instant rice has a firmer grain and slightly cleaner flavor. Which is better is largely personal preference. Hetbahn has more variants and is more widely stocked in the US.

Why did Hetbahn go viral on TikTok?

The "wait, this is microwave rice?" reaction. Americans grew up with Uncle Ben's — cheap, mushy, nothing like real rice. When they tried Hetbahn and got actual fluffy short-grain rice in 90 seconds, the contrast was genuinely shocking. The reaction videos spread widely because the disbelief was authentic.

Is Hetbahn healthy?

White Hetbahn is just plain cooked rice — no preservatives, no additives. Same nutritional profile as a bowl of white rice: ~300 calories per 210g serving. Brown rice and mixed grain versions add more fiber. The Cupban line varies by topping — kimchi stew versions are low in fat but higher in sodium.

What is Cupban (컵반)?

Cupban is CJ's instant meal line — rice with a full stew or sauce topping in one self-contained cup. You peel back the film, microwave, and have a complete meal without extra dishes. Popular flavors include Kimchi Jjigae (김치찌개), Sundubu Jjigae (순두부찌개), and Doenjang Jjigae (된장찌개). It's the most convenient Korean meal you can make at home.
